Before you start
Finding The Theme
  • A theme is the big idea a story wants you to think about — not just what happens.
  • Plot = what happens. Theme = what it means. Mixing them up loses marks.
  • Students say "the theme is love" — but a single word is just a topic, not a theme.
  • Your first question asks if a theme can be a single word like "love" or "death".
In this lecture you will learn
By the end of this lecture, you will know:
  • The difference between a topic (one word) and a theme (a full sentence)
  • How to spot a theme by looking for repeated ideas in a story
  • Why the plot and the theme are not the same thing
  • The three-sentence formula for answering theme questions in exams
