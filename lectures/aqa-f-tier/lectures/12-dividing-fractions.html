Dividing Fractions

Keep the first fraction, flip the second, then multiply

Understanding the Question

Dividing fractions sounds scary, but the trick is simple: division becomes multiplication when you flip the second fraction. This is because dividing asks “how many of these fit into that?”

Reciprocal – The flipped version of a fraction. For example, the reciprocal of 3/5 is 5/3.
Keep-Change-Flip – A memory phrase: keep the first fraction, change ÷ to ×, flip the second fraction.
Multiply – After flipping, you just multiply as normal: top × top, bottom × bottom.

The fundamental skill being tested is whether you can use the reciprocal correctly and then simplify your final answer.

Breaking It Down Step by Step

1
Write the question neatly

Fractions are easy to misread. Neat rewriting reduces cognitive load — less brain power wasted on “what does that say?”.

2
Keep–Change–Flip

Keep the first fraction, change ÷ to ×, flip the second. This creates an automatic routine your memory can grab quickly in exams.

3
Multiply across

Now it’s a normal multiplying fractions question — do top × top and bottom × bottom.

4
Simplify and sanity-check

Simplifying is often required for the accuracy mark. A quick check helps: dividing by a fraction less than 1 usually makes the answer bigger.

Forgetting to change ÷ to × (e.g., writing 3/4 ÷ 5/2). After flipping, it must become multiplication — otherwise you can’t finish the method.
Not simplifying or simplifying incorrectly (e.g., leaving 15/8 when it could be written as 1 7/8 if asked, or reducing using different numbers on top and bottom).

The Method

Step 1
Keep the first fraction the same
Don’t touch the first fraction. Just copy it carefully. Accuracy starts with copying correctly.
Step 2
Change ÷ into ×
Division of fractions is done by multiplying by the reciprocal. So the symbol must change to multiplication.
Step 3
Flip the second fraction (take the reciprocal)
Swap the numerator and denominator of the second fraction. Example: 2/5 becomes 5/2.
Step 4
Multiply and simplify
Multiply across, then simplify. If the question wants a mixed number, convert at the end.

Worked Example

Work out 3/4 ÷ 2/5 and give your answer in its simplest form.
Step 1: Keep–Change–Flip

3/4 ÷ 2/5 becomes 3/4 × 5/2.

Step 2: Multiply across

Numerators: 3 × 5 = 15. Denominators: 4 × 2 = 8. So the answer is 15/8.

Step 3: Simplify / interpret

15/8 is already in simplest form. If needed as a mixed number: 15 ÷ 8 = 1 remainder 7, so 1 7/8.

✓ Before You Submit: Self-Check Checklist

  • Did I keep the first fraction exactly the same?
  • Did I change ÷ to ×?
  • Did I flip only the second fraction?
  • Is my final answer simplified (and a mixed number if the question asks)?
Quick reminder:

Dividing by 1/2 is the same as doubling. That’s a great “sanity check” to see if your answer direction makes sense.

Key Terms Reference

Reciprocal – The flipped fraction (e.g., 2/5 becomes 5/2).
Keep–Change–Flip – Keep the first fraction, change ÷ to ×, flip the second fraction.
Simplest form – A fraction that can’t be reduced further.
Improper fraction – Top is bigger than bottom (e.g., 15/8).
Mixed number – Whole number plus a fraction (e.g., 1 7/8).
Factor – A number that divides exactly into another number (used for simplifying).
