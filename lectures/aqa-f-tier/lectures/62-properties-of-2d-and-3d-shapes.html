Properties of 2D and 3D Shapes

Identify shapes using sides, angles, faces, edges and vertices

Understanding the Question

These questions test whether you recognise shapes by their features, not just their names. It’s about spotting patterns in sides, angles, faces and corners.

2D shapes – Flat shapes with length and width only (like squares and triangles).
3D shapes – Solid shapes with depth (like cubes and cylinders).
Faces – Flat surfaces on a 3D shape.
Edges and vertices – Edges are where faces meet; vertices are the corners.

The fundamental skill being tested is whether you can match properties to the correct shape.

Path to Full Marks: Your Roadmap

This approach stops confusion between similar-looking shapes.

1
Decide if the shape is 2D or 3D

This halves the choices straight away.

2
Count sides or faces carefully

Counting features is more reliable than guessing by appearance.

3
Check angles and symmetry

Right angles and equal sides narrow it down quickly.

4
Name the shape confidently

Using correct mathematical names secures the final mark.

Use what you counted to choose the correct name.
